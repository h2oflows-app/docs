## Overview

The dashboard is the core of H2OFlows. It shows live CFS readings for every gauge on your watchlist, organized by reach or ungrouped, with sparklines, trend indicators, and flow band labels.

## Watchlist

Your watchlist is the set of gauges on a given dashboard. You can:

- Add any USGS or DWR gauge by searching name or site number
- Reorder gauges by drag-and-drop
- Remove gauges you no longer need
- Group gauges under a custom label

Watchlist state is saved to your account and syncs across devices.

## Multi-dashboard support

Each dashboard is a named, independent watchlist. Common uses:

- **Home rivers** — your local runs checked every morning
- **Road trip** — a specific destination you're planning
- **Permit season** — gauges for a specific permit river

Switch dashboards from the header dropdown. Create new dashboards from the same menu.

## Gauge cards

Each card shows:

- **CFS** — current reading from the data source
- **Flow band** — Too Low / Minimum / Fun / Optimal / Pushy / High / Flood
- **Trend** — rising / falling / steady with rate of change
- **Sparkline** — 48-hour CFS history

::callout{icon="i-lucide-info"}
Readings refresh on a short polling interval. The timestamp on each card shows when the gauge was last polled.
::

## Reach-grouped view

When a gauge is linked to reaches in the registry, those reaches appear as sub-rows under the gauge header. Each sub-row shows the reach name and its flow band label — calculated from the same live CFS reading, using that reach's community-defined flow ranges.

This lets you see multiple runs on one gauge at a glance. For example, a single USGS gauge on the Arkansas River may be shared by Browns Canyon, the Numbers, and Royal Gorge — each with different optimal ranges.

## Track It

Click the stopwatch icon on any gauge card to record a timestamped CFS reading for that gauge. Use it at the put-in to log what you actually ran. Readings appear in your Track It history.
