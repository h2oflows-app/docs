## Create an account

Go to [h2oflows.app](https://h2oflows.app) and sign in with Google. That's it — no password, no email confirmation.

Your account stores:
- Named dashboards and watchlist order
- Custom gauge groups
- Flow alert thresholds (Phase 3)

## Add your first gauge

1. Open the dashboard and click **Add Gauge**
2. Search by river name, gauge name, or USGS site number
3. Select a gauge from the results — it's added to your active dashboard immediately

Gauges refresh automatically. The sparkline shows the last 48 hours; the trend arrow shows whether flow is rising or falling.

## Group gauges by reach

When you add a gauge that's linked to a reach in the registry, H2OFlows offers to show it grouped with its reach name and flow band label. Grouped gauges show:

- Reach name and current flow band (Too Low / Optimal / etc.)
- Live CFS with sparkline
- All reaches sharing that gauge under one header

Ungrouped gauges show raw CFS with no band context.

## Create multiple dashboards

Click the dashboard name in the header to open the dashboard switcher. Use **New Dashboard** to create a named dashboard for a different set of rivers — road trips, a different region, or a specific permit run.

Each dashboard has its own watchlist and gauge order.
